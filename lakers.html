Lakers

2018-2020

From 2018 to 2020, LeBron James embarked on a new chapter with the Lakers. After an injury-filled first season, he led them to the 2020 NBA championship. His dominant performances and leadership, especially during the NBA bubble, further cemented his legacy as one of the game's greatest.

2021-2023

From 2021 to 2023, LeBron James continued to defy age, breaking records and maintaining elite performance with the Lakers. Despite injuries and team struggles, he surpassed the all-time scoring record in 2023. His resilience and brilliance on the court solidified his status as an enduring legend in basketball history.

2024

In 2024, LeBron James, still a force on the court, continued to redefine longevity in the NBA. As he led the Lakers with remarkable performances, his pursuit of further championships and records remained unwavering. Even after two decades in the league, LeBron's presence and influence on the game continued to cement his legacy as one of the greatest players in basketball history.

His Legacy

LeBron James' legacy is one of unparalleled greatness, spanning over two decades of dominance in the NBA. From his early days as "The Chosen One" to becoming a four-time champion, his impact on the game goes beyond statistics. LeBron has consistently redefined what it means to excel in the sport, with his leadership, basketball IQ, and relentless work ethic setting a new standard. His ability to perform at an elite level, even in 2024, is something basketball fans should truly appreciate. Adding to his legendary narrative, LeBron now shares the court with his son, Bronny James, on the Lakers—a historic moment in sports. Their father-son partnership is a testament to LeBron’s enduring influence, and it offers a glimpse into the continuation of his basketball legacy.

Reflecting on LeBron's illustrious career, here are some of the remarkable records he holds:

  • Four-time NBA Champion: 2012, 2013, 2016, 2020.
  • Four-time NBA Finals MVP: 2012, 2013, 2016, 2020.
  • Four-time NBA MVP: 2009, 2010, 2012, 2013.
  • Most points in NBA History: 40,474
  • Four-time Olympic medallist
